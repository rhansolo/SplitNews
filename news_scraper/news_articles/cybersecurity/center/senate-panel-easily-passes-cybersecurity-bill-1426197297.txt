WASHINGTON—The Senate Select Committee on Intelligence easily passed a bill that encourages—but doesn’t require—companies to share information about cyberattacks with each other and the federal government, responding to a growing prevalence of data breaches at large U.S. companies.

The 14-1 vote was the first step in what is likely to be a lengthy battle this year over how to prod firms, many of which are skeptical of government data collection, to collaborate more with federal officials to deter attacks.
